CR-210-R0 baseline restoration module (BLR):  application guide
General Description
Cremat's CR-210-R0 is a single channel baseline restoration (BLR) 
module, intended for use with pulsed signals such as those produced by 
nuclear detection instrumentation. The CR-210-R0 corrects the baseline 
shift occurring in AC-coupled pulse amplifiers that may degrade the 
resolution of the acquired pulse height spectra.
An illustration of the problem can be seen in the scope traces below, 
showing the output of a Gaussian shaping amplifier detecting pulses from 
a tail pulse generator. Most pulse detection electronics utilize AC-coupled 
amplification stages. As a result, the DC gain of these shaping amplifiers is 
zero and thus the time averaged output from the amplifier is at ground 
potential. While this is generally not a problem for detection instruments at 
low or perhaps medium count rates, at high count rates the baseline of the 
signal becomes significantly depressed, reducing the pulse height of the 
signals with respect to ground and degrading the pulse height spectrum. 
The scope traces below show the effect of the baseline depression with 
increasing counting rate, and the restorative effects of the CR-210-R0 
BLR. The input of the CR-210-R0 BLR is connected to the output of an 
uncorrected Gaussian shaping amplifier (in this example a CR-200-1s 
shaping amplifier module). As can be seen, the baseline of the signal at the 
CR-210-R0 output remains at ground potential even as the count rate 
increases to 50k/sec.
 
Package Specifications
The CR-210-R0 circuit is contacted via an 8-pin SIP connection (0.100" 
spacing). Pin 1 is marked with a white dot for identification.

Implementation
The CR-210-R0 BLR stage should be configured to follow the shaping amplifier stage. The 
CR-210-R0 can be used with any of Cremat’s shaping amplifiers, preserving the Gaussian 
signals with shaping times from 50 ns through 8 s. Any DC offset present at the shaping 
amplifier output will be lost as the baseline is redefined in the CR-210-R0 circuitry. The 
CR-210-R0 works only with signals containing positive pulses. 
Response to changing count rates
In situations where the count rate is changing, the baseline is of course redefined to 
accommodate this. The scope traces below show how quickly the baseline is redefined as a 
result of increasing count rate (correction increases the baseline with a time constant of 200 
s), and as a result of decreasing count rates (correction decreases the baseline with a time 
constant of 20 ms).
 
BLR Efficacy
The figure on the right shows the 
efficacy of the CR-210-R0 circuit 
when implemented after a CR-200-1s 
shaper amplifier. At count rates of 
approximately 120 kHz or more the 
Gaussian pulses start running together 
and the baseline can no longer be 
preserved.
For other shaping times, this figure can 
be used with the count rate scaled 
proportionately.
